# Chromatic Mastery – Canonical Failure Reasons

## Purpose

Provide a unified set of failure reasons used across all puzzles.

Each reason represents a core concept in color theory and maps to a consistent player-facing explanation.

---

# Core Failure Categories

## 1. VALUE

### low_value_contrast
Light and dark areas are too similar, causing forms to disappear.

### incorrect_value_structure
The overall light/dark arrangement does not match the intended form.

---

## 2. CHROMA

### chroma_collapsed
The color has become dull or muddy due to over-mixing or neutralisation.

### insufficient_chroma
The color lacks intensity compared to the target.

### excessive_chroma
Too many highly saturated colors are competing, reducing clarity.

---

## 3. HUE RELATIONSHIPS

### complement_conflict
Opposing hues are cancelling each other out.

### incorrect_hue_selection
The chosen hue does not match the target or required relationship.

### incorrect_hue_bias
The pigment contains a hidden opposing hue that affects the result.

---

## 4. MIXING & PIGMENT LOGIC

### overmixing
Too many pigments have been combined, reducing clarity and control.

### unbalanced_mix
The ratio of components is incorrect, shifting the result away from the target.

---

## 5. LIGHT & ENVIRONMENT

### insufficient_atmosphere
Distant objects are too clear or saturated, breaking depth illusion.

### excessive_atmosphere
The scene is overly faded, losing necessary contrast and structure.

### incorrect_color_temperature
The overall warmth/coolness does not match the target lighting condition.

---

## 6. PERCEPTION & RELATIVITY

### weak_simultaneous_contrast
Surrounding colors are not strong enough to shift perception.

### competing_focal_points
Too many elements are drawing attention at once.

### weak_accent_isolation
The main color does not stand out from its surroundings.

---

## Usage Rules

- Each puzzle must use only relevant failure reasons
- Prefer 1–3 reasons per failure for clarity
- Order reasons by importance (most critical first)
- Avoid overlapping reasons unless they describe distinct issues

---

## Mapping Guidelines

### Example: Mud Monster
- chroma_collapsed
- complement_conflict
- overmixing

### Example: Vibrant Green
- incorrect_hue_bias
- complement_conflict
- insufficient_chroma

### Example: Depth Painting
- insufficient_atmosphere
- low_value_contrast

---

## Design Principle

Failure reasons should describe:

what went wrong  
why it happened  
what to adjust next  

---

## Success Metric

A player should be able to read the feedback and immediately answer:

"What should I try differently?"
